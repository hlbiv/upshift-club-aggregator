## Approved design (do not re-litigate)

The user approved these four decisions in the conversation immediately preceding this task:

- **Detection approach: hybrid.** Exact-match nav-word list + HTML-element detection (<nav>, <header>, <footer> children). NOT regex, NOT short-string heuristic. The exact-match list is the primary signal (cheap, high-precision). The element-detection is the secondary signal — but only where the scraper pipeline still has raw HTML at the point of detection. Your first reconnaissance step is figuring out whether that's feasible at batch-job time or whether element-detection has to happen inline in the scraper (in which case it writes a side-channel hint into the snapshot that the batch job reads).
- **Per-snapshot flag granularity.** Already the shipped schema — keep as is.
- **Resolve-only for Phase 2.** Button marks a flag resolved. No allowlist, no suppress-permanently semantics. Allowlisting is a Phase 3 decision if false positives prove real.
- **Batch job pattern.** Modeled on canonical-schools-linker. Scheduled via .replit cron at something like 30 3 * * * UTC, invoked via python3 run.py --source nav-leaked-names-detect.
